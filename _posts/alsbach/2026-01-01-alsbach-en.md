## Alsbach Castle (Alsbacher Schloss)
![Schloss Alsbach]({{ site.baseurl }}/_image/Alsbach/Alsbach.jpg)
Alsbach Castle (also known as Bickenbach Castle, or Burg Bickenbach) is situated on a hill overlooking the village of Alsbach. Its history dates back to 1235, and it is believed to have been built by Gottfried I von Bickenbach.

The castle remained inhabited until around 1664. Over the following two centuries, this once-magnificent fortress was used as a quarry by local residents, serving as a source of free stone and falling into a state of ruin. It was not until 1863, when Grand Duke Ludwig II of Hesse ordered restoration and reinforcement work, that the castle was saved from total collapse and preserved for posterity.

In 1997, the Historical and Cultural Association of Alsbach Castle was founded with the support of the State of Hesse. The association is dedicated to the maintenance of the castle and frequently hosts events here, inviting visitors to explore its rich history and enduring charm.
