## History of Görlitz
![Görlitz Peterskirche]({{ site.baseurl }}/_image/Görlitz/Görlitz.jpg)
Görlitz is the easternmost city in Germany. It was first mentioned in a document in 1073, when the Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV gifted the village of "Goreliz" to the Bishop of Meissen.

Situated on the Via Regia (the King's Road), Görlitz gradually developed into a merchant settlement centered around St. Nicholas Church (Nikolaikirche) during the mid-12th century, becoming a major medieval trading hub over the following centuries.

Görlitz suffered minimal damage during World War II, leaving it with one of the best-preserved old towns in Central Europe, boasting nearly 4,000 heritage sites. This architectural beauty has attracted numerous film productions, earning the city the nickname "Görliwood." Famous films such as The Book Thief and The Grand Budapest Hotel were filmed here.

## Görlitz Railway Station (Bahnhof Görlitz)
![Görlitz Bahnhof]({{ site.baseurl }}/_image/Görlitz/Görlitz_Bf.jpg)
Construction began in 1845, and the Görlitz Railway Station opened in 1847, giving it a history of over 170 years. Until World War II, it served as a vital transport hub connecting Berlin and Dresden.

On a cloudy day, the Görlitz station exudes a unique and evocative atmosphere.

## Görlitz Department Store (Kaufhaus Görlitz)
![Görlitz Kaufhaus]({{ site.baseurl }}/_image/Görlitz/Görlitz_Kaufhaus.jpg)
One of the most famous landmarks in Görlitz is undoubtedly the filming location for the movie The Grand Budapest Hotel 🏨. The real-life setting for the hotel is the Kaufhaus Görlitz. Its predecessor was indeed a hotel (the "Goldener Strauß" or Golden Ostrich), which was later rebuilt as a department store in 1912 and opened in 1913.

After a series of ownership changes, the Görlitz department store finally closed its doors in August 2009. Renovation plans are currently still in the works.

Beyond the main building itself, you can also find various filming locations for The Grand Budapest Hotel tucked away in the narrow alleys throughout the city of Görlitz!
